# Research For Examples of Gaza Media Coverage That Prove Pro-Israel Bias

For many consumers of U.S. news, the premise that media organizations are biased against Palestinians — and systematically favor Israelis — is simply taken for granted. The premise, however, is rarely, if ever, empirically and quantitatively shown to be true, because doing so can be very time-consuming; it’s complex and full of pitfalls and nuances that can muddy the picture. Yet the double standards are everywhere — and there are key ways one can do comparative media analysis to show how Israeli and Palestinian life, as well as how other recent conflicts, are covered.

In his new book “[How to Sell a Genocide: The Media’s Complicity in the Destruction of Gaza],”(https://www.plutobooks.com/product/how-to-sell-a-genocide/) Adam Johnson attempts to show, beyond a reasonable doubt, that U.S. media coverage of the Israel’s war on Gaza was demonstrably one-sided, racist, dehumanizing, and often veered into outright incitement.

In Johnson’s analysis, he examined over 12,000 articles from the New York Times, the Washington Post, CNN.com, Politico, Axios, USA Today, the Associated Press, and the Los Angeles Times, along with 5,000 TV segments that aired on CNN and MSNBC. The focus is on the first year of the conflict — zooming in on the first few months after October 7, 2023, when the premises of Israel’s genocide were quickly codified, rendering mass death inevitable.
