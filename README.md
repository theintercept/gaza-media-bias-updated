# Research For Examples of Gaza Media Coverage That Prove Pro-Israel Bias

For many consumers of U.S. news, the premise that media organizations are biased against Palestinians — and systematically favor Israel — is simply taken for granted. The premise, however, is rarely, if ever, empirically and quantitatively shown to be true, because doing so can be very time-consuming, complex and full of pitfalls and nuances that can muddy the picture.Yet the double standards are everywhere — and there are ways to do sober there are key ways one can do comparative media analysis to show double standards not only in how Israeli and Palestinian life are covered, but how other recent conflicts can help illuminate this reality further. 

For Adam Johnson's new book [“How to Sell a Genocide: The Media’s Complicity in the Destruction of Gaza,”](https://www.plutobooks.com/product/how-to-sell-a-genocide/) he attempt to show, beyond a reasonable doubt. that U,S. media coverage of the war on Gaza was demonstrably one-sided, racist, dehumanizing, and often veered into outright incitement. 

In Johnson's analysis, he examined over 12,000 articles from the New York Times, the Washington Post, CNN.com, Politico, Axios, USA Today, and the Associate Press, along with 5,000 TV segments that aired on CNN and MSNBC. The focus is on the first year of the conflict — with an emphasis on he first few months, when the premises of genocide were quickly codified, rendering mass death inevitable.
